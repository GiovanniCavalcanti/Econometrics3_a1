April 30, 2003

Stock and Watson Indicator Report

February 2003


The Experimental Index of Coincident Indicators (XCI) was 232.8 in February, down from its January value of 233.5, based on the most recently available data.  During the three months from November 2002 to February 2003 the XCI fell by 1.0% at an annual rate.  During the previous three months from August to November, the XCI fell by 0.7% at an annual rate.  Over the entire six months from August to February, the XCI therefore fell 0.9% at an annual rate.


A calculation based on the variables in the XCI indicates that the probability that the economy was in a recession in February is 28%.  Historical values of this Experimental Concident Recession Index (XRI-C) are shown in Figure 4.


The Experimental Leading Index (XLI) for February is 2.2.  This index, based on seven leading indicators, is a forecast of the percentage growth of the XCI (at an annual rate) over the six months from February to August 2003.


Of the indicators used to calculate the XLI, five made a positive contribution to the index, relative to trend:  housing authorizations (building permits);  exchange rates;  the yield on 10-year U.S. Treasury bonds;  the premium on 3-month commercial paper over 3-month U.S. Treasury bills;  and the spread between the yield on 10-year vs. 1-year U.S. Treasury bonds.  Two of the indicators made a negative contribution to the index, relative to trend:  manufacturers' unfilled orders;  and part-time employment due to slack work.


Based on the most recent data, the Experimental Recession Index (XRI), which estimates the probability that the economy will be in a recession six months later, was 10% in February indicating a 10% probability that the economy will be in a recession in August 2003.  The alternative experimental recession index (the XRI-2), which is based on seven leading indicators that do not include interest rates or interest rate spreads, estimates a 5% probability that the economy will be in a recession in August 2003.
