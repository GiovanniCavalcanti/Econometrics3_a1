April 16, 2004

Stock and Watson Indicator Report

 

December 2003

 

The Experimental Index of Coincident Indicators (XCI) fell slightly to 238.6 in December 2003 from 238.8 in November, based on the most recently available data. During the three months from September to December the XCI rose by 3.2% at an annual rate. During the three months from June to September, the XCI rose by 4.0% at an annual rate.  Over the six months from June to December, the XCI therefore rose by 3.6% at an annual rate.

 

A calculation based on the variables in the XCI indicates that the probability that the economy was in a recession in December is 3%. Historical values of this Experimental Coincident Recession Index (XRI-C) are shown in Figure 4.

 

The Experimental Leading Index (XLI) for December is 3.1. This index, based on seven leading indicators, is a forecast of the percentage growth of the XCI (at an annual rate) over the six months from December 2003 to June 2004.

 

Of the indicators used to calculate the XLI, all made a positive contribution to the index, relative to trend: housing authorizations (building permits); manufacturers' unfilled orders; exchange rates;  part-time employment due to slack work: the yield on 10-year U.S. Treasury bonds; the premium on 3-month commercial paper over 3-month U.S. Treasury bills; and the spread between the yield on 10-year vs. 1-year U.S. Treasury bonds.

 

Based on the most recent data, the Experimental Recession Index (XRI), which estimates the probability that the economy will be in a recession six months later, was 7% in December indicating a 7% probability that the economy will be in a recession in June 2004. The alternative experimental recession index (the XRI-2), which is based on seven leading indicators that do not include interest rates or interest rate spreads, estimates a 5% probability that the economy will be in a recession in June 2004.
