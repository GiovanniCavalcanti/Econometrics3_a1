August 9, 2002

Stock and Watson Indicator Report

June 2002


The Experimental Index of Coincident Indicators (XCI) rose to 239.2 in June from its May value of 237.9, based on the most recently available data.  During the three months from March to June the XCI rose by 3.6% at an annual rate.  During the previous three months from December to March, the XCI rose by 1.7% at an annual rate.  Over the entire six months from December to June, the XCI therefore rose 2.6% at an annual rate.


A calculation based on the variables in the XCI indicates that the probability that the economy was in a recession in June is 1%.  Historical values of this Experimental Concident Recession Index (XRI-C) are shown in Figure 4.


The Experimental Leading Index (XLI) for June is 4.2.  This index, based on seven leading indicators, is a forecast of the percentage growth of the XCI (at an annual rate) over the next six months, from June to December 2002.


Of the indicators used to calculate the XLI, five  made a positive contribution to the index, relative to trend:  housing authorizations (building permits);  part-time employment due to slack work;  the yield on 10-year U.S. Treasury bonds;  the premium on 3-month commercial paper over 3-month U.S. Treasury bills;  and the spread between the yield on 10-year vs. 1-year U.S. Treasury bonds.  One of the indicators, manufacturers' unfilled orders, made a negative contribution to the index, relative to trend, and another, exchange rates, made no net contribution, relative to trend.


Based on the most recent data, the Experimental Recession Index (XRI), which estimates the probability that the economy will be in a recession six months later, was 2% in June, indicating a 2% probability that the economy will be in a recession in December 2002.  The alternative experimental recession index (the XRI-2), which is based on seven leading indicators that do not include interest rates or interest rate spreads, estimates a 7% probability that the economy will be in a recession in December 2002.
