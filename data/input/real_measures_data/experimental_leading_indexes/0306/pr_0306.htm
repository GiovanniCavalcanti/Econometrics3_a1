August 5, 2003

Stock and Watson Indicator Report

 

June 2003

 

The Experimental Index of Coincident Indicators (XCI) held constant at 231.5 in June, based on the most recently available data. During the three months from March to June the XCI fell by 0.5% at an annual rate. During the three months from December to March, the XCI fell by 1.0% at an annual rate.  Over the six months from December to June, the XCI therefore fell 0.8% at an annual rate.

 

A calculation based on the variables in the XCI indicates that the probability that the economy was in a recession in June is 28%. Historical values of this Experimental Coincident Recession Index (XRI-C) are shown in Figure 4.  The probabilities of the XRI-C align closely with the December, 2001 turning point recently announced by the NBER Business Cycle Dating Committee.

 

The Experimental Leading Index (XLI) for June is 3.4. This index, based on seven leading indicators, is a forecast of the percentage growth of the XCI (at an annual rate) over the six months from June to December.

 

Of the indicators used to calculate the XLI, all made a positive contribution to the index, relative to trend: housing authorizations (building permits); manufacturers' unfilled orders; exchange rates; part-time employment due to slack work; the yield on 10-year U.S. Treasury bonds; the premium on 3-month commercial paper over 3-month U.S. Treasury bills; and the spread between the yield on 10-year vs. 1-year U.S. Treasury bonds.

 

Based on the most recent data, the Experimental Recession Index (XRI), which estimates the probability that the economy will be in a recession six months later, was 6% in June indicating a 6% probability that the economy will be in a recession in December 2003. The alternative experimental recession index (the XRI-2), which is based on seven leading indicators that do not include interest rates or interest rate spreads, estimates a 7% probability that the economy will be in a recession in December 2003.
