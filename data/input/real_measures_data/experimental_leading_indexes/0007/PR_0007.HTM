August 29, 2000

Stock and Watson Indicator Report

July 2000
The Experimental Index of Coincident Indicators (XCI) rose to 244.6 in July from 244.1 in June, based on the most recently available data. During the three months from April to July, the XCI grew 1.7% at an annual rate, while during the three months from January to April, the XCI grew 4.4% at an annual rate. Over the six months from January to July, the XCI grew 3.0% at an annual rate.

The Experimental Leading Index (XLI) for July is 4.1. This index, based on seven leading indicators, is a forecast of the percentage growth of the XCI (at an annual rate) over the next six months, from July to January 2001. The July XLI forecasts a growth rate one percentage point above that of the past six months.

Of the indicators used to calculate the XLI, two made a positive contribution to the index, relative to trend: manufacturers' unfilled orders; and yield on 10-year U.S. Treasury bonds. Four of the indicators made negative contributions to the index, relative to trend: housing authorizations (building permits); part-time employment due to slack work; the premium on 3-month commercial paper over 3-month U.S. Treasury bills; and the spread between the yield on 10-year vs. 1-year U.S. Treasury bonds. Exchange rates made no net contribution, relative to trend.

Based on the most recent data, the Experimental Recession Index (XRI), which estimates the probability that the economy will be in a recession six months later, was 2% in July, indicating a 2% probability that the economy will be in a recession in January 2001.

The alternative experimental recession index (the XRI-2), which is based on seven leading indicators that do not include interest rates or interest rate spreads, estimates a 8% probability that the economy will be in a recession in January 2001.

Figure 4 shows the probability that the economy will be in a recession during each of the months from July to January 2001, computed using data through July. Based on the data through July for the variables in the XRI, the probability that the economy was in a recession in July is 1%, the probability of being in a recession in August is 1%, and the probability of being in a recession in January is 2%. Based on the variables in the XRI-2, the probability that the economy was in a recession in July is 1%, the probability of being in a recession in August is 2%, and the probability of being in a recession in January is 8%.
