July 10, 2002

Stock and Watson Indicator Report

May 2002


The Experimental Index of Coincident Indicators (XCI) rose to 239.1 in May from its April value of 238.8, based on the most recently available data.  During the three months from February to May the XCI rose by 1.9% at an annual rate.  During the previous three months from November to February, the XCI rose by 2.0% at an annual rate.  Over the entire six months from November to May, the XCI therefore rose 2.0% at an annual rate.


A calculation based on the variables in the XCI indicates that the probability that the economy was still in recession in May is 3%.  Historical values of this Experimental Concident Recession Index (XRI-C) are shown in Figure 4.


The Experimental Leading Index (XLI) for May is 3.3.  This index, based on seven leading indicators, is a forecast of the percentage growth of the XCI (at an annual rate) over the next six months, from May to November 2002.


Of the indicators used to calculate the XLI, three made a positive contribution to the index, relative to trend: part-time employment due to slack work;  the premium on 3-month commercial paper over 3-month U.S. Treasury bills;  and the spread between the yield on 10-year vs. 1-year U.S. Treasury bonds.  Two of the indicators made a negative contribution to the index, relative to trend:  housing authorizations (building permits); and manufacturers' unfilled orders.  Two indicators made no net contribution, relative to trend:  exchange rates;  and the yield on 10-year U.S. Treasury bonds.


Based on the most recent data, the Experimental Recession Index (XRI), which estimates the probability that the economy will be in a recession six months later, was 3% in May, indicating a 3% probability that the economy will be in a recession in November 2002.  The alternative experimental recession index (the XRI-2), which is based on seven leading indicators that do not include interest rates or interest rate spreads, estimates a 7% probability that the economy will be in a recession in November 2002.
