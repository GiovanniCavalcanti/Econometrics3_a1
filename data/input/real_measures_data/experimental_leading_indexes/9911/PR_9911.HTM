January 3, 2000

Stock and Watson Indicator Report

November 1999
The Experimental Index of Coincident Indicators (XCI) rose to 237.7 in November from 236.6 in October and 235.0 in September, based on the most recently available data. Over the three months from August to November, the XCI grew 4.0% at an annual rate, while during the three months from May to August, the XCI grew 4.7% at an annual rate. Over the six months from May to November, the XCI grew 4.3% at an annual rate.

The Experimental Leading Index (XLI) for November is 2.8. This index, based on seven leading indicators, is a forecast of the percentage growth of the XCI (at an annual rate) over the next six months, from November to May 2000. Because XCI growth has averaged approximately 3% annually since 1960, the November XLI forecasts growth at approximately its historical average rate.

Based on the most recent data, the Experimental Recession Index (XRI), which estimates the probability that the economy will be in a recession six months later, was 6% in November, indicating a 6% probability that the economy will be in a recession in May 2000.

Of the indicators used to calculate the XLI, two made a positive contribution to the index, relative to trend: housing authorizations (building permits); and exchange rates. Five of the indicators made negative contributions to the index, relative to trend: manufacturers' unfilled orders; part-time employment due to slack work; the yield on 10-year U.S. Treasury bonds; the premium on 3-month commercial paper over 3-month U.S. Treasury bills; and the spread between the yield on 10-year vs. 1-year U.S. Treasury bonds.

The alternative experimental recession index (the XRI-2), which is based on seven leading indicators that do not include interest rates or interest rate spreads, estimates a 6% probability that the economy will be in a recession in May 2000, the same as the XRI.

Figure 4 shows the probability that the economy will be in a recession during each of the months from November 1999 to May 2000, computed using data through November. Based on the data through November for the variables in the XRI, the probability that the economy was in a recession in November is 1%, the probability of being in a recession in December is 1%, and the probability of being in a recession in May is 6%. Based on the variables in the XRI-2, the probability that the economy was in a recession in November is 1%, the probability of being in a recession in December is 1%, and the probability of being in a recession in May is 6%.
