February 13, 2003

Stock and Watson Indicator Report

December 2002


The Experimental Index of Coincident Indicators (XCI) was 232.9 in December, down from its November value of 233.5, based on the most recently available data.  During the three months from September to December the XCI fell by 1.4% at an annual rate.  During the previous three months from June to September, the XCI rose by 0.5% at an annual rate.  Over the entire six months from June to December, the XCI therefore fell 0.4% at an annual rate.


A calculation based on the variables in the XCI indicates that the probability that the economy was in a recession in December is 29%.  Historical values of this Experimental Concident Recession Index (XRI-C) are shown in Figure 4.


The Experimental Leading Index (XLI) for December is 3.7.  This index, based on seven leading indicators, is a forecast of the percentage growth of the XCI (at an annual rate) over the six months from December 2002 to June 2003.


Of the indicators used to calculate the XLI, four made a positive contribution to the index, relative to trend:  housing authorizations (building permits);  part-time employment due to slack work;  the premium on 3-month commercial paper over 3-month U.S. Treasury bills;  and the spread between the yield on 10-year vs. 1-year U.S. Treasury bonds.  Two of the indicators made a negative contribution to the index, relative to trend:   exchange rates;  and the yield on 10-year U.S. Treasury bonds.  One indicator, manufacturers' unfilled orders, made no net contribution, relative to trend.


Based on the most recent data, the Experimental Recession Index (XRI), which estimates the probability that the economy will be in a recession six months later, was 6% in December indicating a 6% probability that the economy will be in a recession in June 2003.  The alternative experimental recession index (the XRI-2), which is based on seven leading indicators that do not include interest rates or interest rate spreads, estimates a 5% probability that the economy will be in a recession in June 2003.
